Methodology

I use K-Means, Spherical K-Means Algorithm and Gibs Sampling Algorithm to cluster the posts.

####K-means


    K-Means is the most popular algorithm in the cluster problem.It’s a good choice to start our project:



Figure7. K-Means Result
The posts may clustered the paitients into three level:

- Light: help, year, life, know, many
- Mild: pain, face, like, help, would
- Severe: disease, suicide, suicide disease

The K-Means is good in unsupervised cluster, but the different length of Feature Vector will lead to a big distance.
Adam: TN disease makes me pain on face.
Bob: I feel suicide pain on my face since I have TN.
Two Post have the same meanings:
But the Feature Vector of them are:
Name |pain |face|suicide |feel |
——–|—–|—-|——–|—–|
Adam | 0.01|0.01|0 |0 |
Bob | 0.01|0.01|0.005 |0.005|

It’s apparently the distance of Euclidean Distance depends on the length of Posts.
So there’s another algorithm called Spherical-Kmeans to solove this problem.
Spherical K-Means uses Cosine Similarity instead of Euclidean distance. You can imagine the cosine similarity as the Angel distance.



Figure8. Spherical K-Means Result



Figure8. Spherical K-Means Result fits the Spherical Shape

From the figure above we find that the Spherical have a good perforamnce. Each cluster has distinguish key-word-vector. But when the samples numbers is greater than 300. K-means and Spherical K-Means both don’t help the sparse matrix cluster.
